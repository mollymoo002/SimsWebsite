The Sims

The Sims was released in 2000 for Windows and featured 7 expansion packs and 2 deluxe editions of the game. By 2002, the game sold more than 6.3 million copies worldwide: putting it at the best-selling PC game of all time.

This was my first time playing The Sims and I fell in love instantly. Controlling virtual people in their everyday lives and doing things like going to work, decorating a house, marrying another sim, and having kids was so much fun for a younger me. My favorite expansion for The Sims was The Sims Makin' Magic and I could play it all day long.

Here are the Expansion packs with some brief descriptions of gameplay

The Sims: Livin' Large

The game adds new non playable characters like Servo the robot, the Tragic Clown, Santa Clause, and the Grim Reaper. The pack is no specifically themed, but it does add some quality of life improvements like adding more than one neighborhood, cockroaches, magic lamp, and genies.

The Sims: House Party

This expansion adds the ability to have parties at their house and if the party is fun enough, Drew Carey in sim form appears to join in on the fun.

The Sims: Hot Date

Hot Date adds a new destination called Downtown where sims can go on outings or dates. It also updated the relationship system for long-term and short-term relationships.

The Sims: Vacation

Adds a new destination called Vacation Island where sims can take vacations in the mountains, in the woods, and at the beach.

The Sims: Unleashed

This my least favorite expansion pack. This adds various pets to the game like cats and dogs you can adopt.

The Sims: Superstar

This expansion pack adds the ability for your sims to become entertainers and celebrities will visit your game in sim form to be interacted with. Some of the celebrities include Avril Lavigne, Andy Warhol, Freddie Prinze, etc.

The Sims: Makin' Magic

Makin' Magic introduces magic to the game and allows sims to cast spells, forge charms, and practice alchemy.
